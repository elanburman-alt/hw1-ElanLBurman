## Leveraging AI for Better Donor Retention in the Hospital and Health System Setting

Donor retention is one of the most persistent challenges I face in healthcare philanthropy. Unlike a university that can rally alumni around homecoming or a museum that draws members back with new exhibitions, a hospital's relationship with a grateful patient or family member can feel transactional once the care episode ends. Building lasting loyalty requires us to continually demonstrate impact, deepen personal connections, and make every donor feel seen — and that is incredibly hard to do at scale with lean development teams. I believe generative AI can change the equation in meaningful ways.

- **Intelligent stewardship journeys.** AI can help me design and automate individualized stewardship tracks that adapt over time — adjusting the cadence, content, and channel of touchpoints based on how each donor responds, so that a first-time grateful-patient donor and a longtime major-gift supporter each receive an experience that feels intentionally crafted for them.
- **Early warning systems for donor attrition.** By analyzing giving patterns, event attendance, email engagement, and even publicly available life-change signals, AI models can flag donors who are likely to lapse well before they stop giving, giving my team the chance to intervene with a personal call or visit rather than a generic re-solicitation letter.
- **Impact reporting that resonates.** Generative AI can help me transform raw program data — patient outcomes, community health metrics, capital project milestones — into compelling, donor-facing impact reports tailored to the specific fund or initiative each donor supports, making the connection between their gift and real-world results feel vivid and immediate.

## Using AI to Better Target the Timing, Nature, and Scope of Donor Communications

In fundraising, the right message delivered at the wrong time — or the wrong message at the right time — can be just as ineffective as no outreach at all. I spend a great deal of energy thinking about when to reach out to a donor, what to say, and how ambitious the ask should be. Too often, those decisions are driven by gut instinct or rigid annual calendars rather than by real signals from the donor. Generative AI offers a way to bring much more precision and personalization to this critical part of my work.

- **Optimal send-time modeling.** AI can analyze historical open rates, response patterns, and even external factors like fiscal year-end cycles or news events related to healthcare to recommend the ideal moment to deliver a solicitation, update, or thank-you — increasing the likelihood that my message actually gets read and acted upon.
- **Dynamic message framing.** Rather than sending the same appeal letter to every donor on a list, I can use generative AI to produce multiple versions of a communication, each framed around the themes, programs, or stories most likely to resonate with a particular donor segment, all while maintaining a consistent organizational voice.
- **Right-sized ask amounts.** AI tools can evaluate a donor's giving history, capacity indicators, engagement trajectory, and peer benchmarks to suggest an ask amount that is ambitious enough to grow the relationship but calibrated enough to avoid donor fatigue — a balance I currently strike through experience and intuition, but that could be sharpened considerably with data-driven guidance.

## The Use of AI in Healthcare Broadly to Reduce Costs, Increase Quality, and Drive Access

Beyond my own work in philanthropy, I find myself deeply excited — and, as someone who works inside a health system, deeply invested — in the broader potential of AI to reshape healthcare delivery. The challenges are enormous: costs continue to rise, quality remains uneven, and millions of people still struggle to access the care they need. These are problems that no single innovation will solve, but I believe AI is one of the most promising tools we have to make meaningful progress on all three fronts simultaneously.

- **Administrative cost reduction through automation.** A staggering share of healthcare spending goes to administrative tasks — prior authorizations, claims processing, clinical documentation, scheduling. Generative AI and intelligent automation can streamline these workflows, freeing up clinician time for patient care and reducing the overhead costs that ultimately get passed along to patients and payers.
- **Clinical decision support for better outcomes.** AI models trained on vast datasets of patient records, imaging, and genomic information can surface patterns that help clinicians catch diseases earlier, choose more effective treatments, and reduce diagnostic errors — raising the overall quality of care without necessarily adding cost.
- **Expanding access through virtual care and triage.** AI-powered chatbots, symptom checkers, and virtual health assistants can serve as a front door to the health system for people in underserved or rural communities, helping them navigate their options, answer basic health questions, and connect with the right provider at the right time — breaking down barriers of geography, language, and health literacy that have historically kept too many people from getting the care they deserve.
